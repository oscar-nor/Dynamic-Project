Multidimensional Poverty in Mexico

By Oscar Noriega

Probably the main, and most discussed, public policy issue in Mexico is poverty alleviation. However, the concept of poverty comprises various components or dimensions, that is, it is a multi-dimensional phenomenon that cannot be considered, solely and exclusively, by the goods and services that can be purchased in the market (ie. income measurement alone).

The goal of this project is to familiarize yourself briefly with the concept of multidimensional poverty measurement and its main difference with the normal income measurements. Classical poverty measurement

Historically, the main indicator for measuring poverty was just a headcount of people who earned an income below a 'Poverty line'.

While this indicator has its advantages, one of the main ones being the ability to compare between different countries, these measurements doesn't account for the different contexts (even if using Purchase Power Parity, or PPP).

The chart on the left shows the comparison of Mexico vs another countries/zones, using a poverty line of $5.50 USD PPP by the World Bank aimed at approaching an international context of how Mexico can be compared, with its proper cautions, with the rest of the world.

Multidimensional measurement

CONEVAL defined a second dimension: Social Rights Deprivations. Based on six social rights: education, health, social security, housing, basic housing services and feeding. Combining this with monthly income measurement, CONEVAL defined 5 categories to classify the population:

1- Extreme Poverty: Having an income below the Minimum Welfare Line (MWL) and 3 or more Social Deprivations (SD).

2- Moderate Poverty: Having an income below the Economic Welfare Line (EWL) but above the MWL and at least 1 SDs, or an income below the MWL and no more that two SDs.

3- Income vulnerability: Income below the EWL, but no SDs.

4- Social Deprivations vulnerability: Income above the EWL, and at least 1 SD.

5- Non poor or vulnerable: Income above the EWL, and no SDs.

Extreme Poverty Evolution by State

Now let's start seeing the heterogeneity of poverty evolution inside Mexico, by looking at its states.

Extreme Poverty, by construction, represents the most vulnerable portion of he population. These are mexicans whose income doesn't allow them to buy the Basic Food Basket.

In the last years we can see that in most states, there was a reduction in Extreme Poverty rates (something we already knew from the previous aggregate chart).

However, it's important to notice that some southern states saw a considerable increase, particularly Oaxaca, Veracruz and Morelos contrast with the rest of the Republic.

Moderate Poverty Evolution by State

Unlike Extreme poverty, we can see a more hetrogeneous evolution of mdoerate poverty rates by states.

On this map is clearer the North/South difference, where the South concentrates largest poverty rates compared tp the North.

It's highly likely that the people who moved out from extreme poverty went to moderate in the South states.

It's important to notice that, when refering to just 'Poverty rates' in Mexico, these are usually understood as the sum of Extreme and Moderate poverty rates. However, given the difference and the relevant reduction in Extreme poverty, we show both as a further reinforcement of the states heterogeneity and the multidimensionality measurement.

Social Deprivations evolution by State

In terms of Social Deprivations vulnerability, we surprisingly see that one of the states with the lowest poverty levels, Baja California Sur, had the largest increase of population showing at least one social deprivation between 2012 and 2018.

Unexpected as well, Chiapas and Veracruz, two of the states with the highest poverty increases, are the states with the largest reduction of popuation having social deprivations. This reinforces a hypothesis that low income almost exclusively determines social deprivations on poorer states.

This implies that people with income below the poverty lines are unlikely to show social deprivations (on average)

Income Vulnerability evolution by State

Kind of expected, we see that the northern states have the highest negative percentage change of population having a low income, but no social deprivations.

This trend of income vulnerability reduction seems to have taken place at almost all states. However, the three states that concentrate the largest poverty rates (Chiapas, Oaxaca and Guerrero, clearly distinguable in the map) went against that nationwide trend

The story told by all the previous maps show us that in the North there's a broader access, and recent improvement, to the Social Rights defined by CONEVAL, even to people with low income, while in the South (at least the three poorer states) the social deprivations are almost solely for people with low income.
